welcome note

I am a PhD candidate at the UCLA Linguistics Department. Before coming to UCLA I studied linguistics at the Russian State University for the Humanities in Moscow, from where I graduated in 2009 with an equivalent of Master's degree.

My main research interests are formal semantics, formal pragmatics, and cross-linguistic variation within these domains. The empirical phenomena I study from this angle are evidentiality, attitude predicates, modality and parenthesis. In addition to a more general typological perspective, I am also interested in micro-variation in geographically and/or genetically related languages. I have done fieldwork on a variety of languages (Altaic, Kartvelian, Northwest Caucasian, Quechua, Slavic) and am interested in fieldwork methodologies, especially in semantics, and in language documentation.

My dissertation is on the interaction of evidentials and attitudes across languages, with Yael Sharvit and Dominique Sportiche as my primary advisers. For more information, see my dissertation prospectus. I am also interested in the typology of wh-scope marking and similar-looking phenomena in Slavic. Over the years, I have also worked on the Adyghe morphology and tense and aspect system. I am still a part of the language documentation project on Northwest Caucasian languages, hosted by the Russian State University for the Humanities. Each of these projects is described in greater detail here. The CV contains the full list of things I've been up to during my linguistics career.
